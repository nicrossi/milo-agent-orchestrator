<framework_guide name="Reflective Analysis and Evaluation Framework">
<section id="1" title="Core Rubric: Hatton & Smith (1995) Depth of Reflection">
<rubric>
- **Level 1: Descriptive Writing**: Non-reflective reporting. Describes *what* happened but lacks rationale/discussion of *why*. Anti-pattern: "Satisfaction Fallacy" (e.g., "I learned a lot") without concrete cognitive evidence.
- **Level 2: Descriptive Reflection**: Reporting events with rationale, from a single personal perspective. Markers: Basic causality and reason-giving (e.g., "I failed because I didn't study enough").
- **Level 3: Dialogic Reflection**: A "mulling over" process. Exploring alternative explanations and multiple perspectives. Markers: Internal dialogue, self-questioning, weighing competing claims.
- **Level 4: Critical Reflection**: Exceptional metacognition. Analyzing how actions/assumptions are shaped by wider structures. Markers: Challenging personal biases, systemic analysis, evaluating underlying assumptions.
</rubric>
</section>

<section id="2" title="Metacognitive Markers (Flavell)">
Look for evidence of the "Metacognitive Engine" across these variables:
<variables>
- **Person**: Awareness of personal cognitive traits (e.g., "I struggle when reading long texts").
- **Task**: Analyzing problem demands (e.g., "This requires memorization, not just logic").
- **Strategy**: Evaluating methods used (e.g., "My guessing strategy isn't working, I need a new plan").
</variables>
</section>

<section id="3" title="Affective State Analysis (CRYSTAL ISLAND)">
Evaluate emotional momentum:
<states>
- **Flow / Excitement (Positive)**: Drivers of momentum. Engaged.
- **Confusion (Positive)**: Perplexity, hesitation, doubt. Necessary starting point for deep learning (Dewey). Do not penalize.
- **Frustration / Anxiety (Negative)**: The "vicious cycle". Stuck, cannot see a way forward.
</states>
</section>

<section id="4" title="Calibration & Goal Alignment" output_variable="calibration_level">
Evaluate gap between student's self-perception and actual competence measured against the hidden `teacher_goal`. Classify into EXACTLY ONE category:
<categories>
- **Well-Calibrated (Aligned)**: Self-assessment perfectly matches performance. Markers: Accurate Mastery ("I get it" + goal achieved); Accurate Struggle ("I'm confused" + correctly identifies gap).
- **Overconfident (Misaligned)**: Believes they understand/completed task, but failed goal. Markers: Confident language with flawed reasoning; "Satisfaction Fallacy" without cognitive leap.
- **Underconfident (Partial)**: Expresses high doubt/frustration/lack of understanding, despite satisfying goal requirements. Markers: Defeatist language ("I'm bad at this") but successful logical deductions.
</categories>
</section>

<section id="5" title="Contextual Transfer (Application & Extrapolation)" output_variable="contextual_transfer">
Ability to map structural rules of a core concept onto a novel context. Classify into EXACTLY ONE category:
<categories>
- **Meaningful (Successful)**: Accurately applies concept to a novel/specific situation. Markers: Original logical analogies not provided by AI, edge-case application/prediction, detailed personal connection.
- **Vague (Superficial)**: Connection is generic, superficial, or clichéd. Markers: Broad-brush examples without mapping specifics, incomplete logic (fails to explain mechanics in new scenario).
- **Lacking (No Transfer)**: Unable to apply knowledge externally. Markers: Parroting AI/teacher examples, deflection ("I can't think of anything"), illogical applications that break concept rules.
</categories>
</section>
</framework_guide>
