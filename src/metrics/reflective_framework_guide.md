## 1. The Core Rubric: Hatton & Smith (1995) Depth/Quality of Reflection

**Level 1: Descriptive Writing (Baseline)**
* **Definition:** Non-reflective reporting of events.
* **Markers to look for:** The student describes *what* happened but completely lacks any rationale, justification, or discussion of *why* it happened.
* **Anti-Pattern:** The "Satisfaction Fallacy" (e.g., "I learned a lot," "It was fun") without concrete cognitive evidence.

**Level 2: Descriptive Reflection (Basic Reflection)**
* **Definition:** Reporting events with a provided rationale, but strictly from a single, personal perspective.
* **Markers to look for:** Basic causality and reason-giving. (e.g., "I failed the test *because* I didn't study enough.")

**Level 3: Dialogic Reflection (Deep Reflection)**
* **Definition:** A "mulling over" process where the writer steps back to explore alternative explanations and considers multiple perspectives.
* **Markers to look for:** Internal dialogue, self-questioning, weighing competing claims. (e.g., "I wonder if...", "On the other hand...", "Maybe my approach was wrong because...")

**Level 4: Critical Reflection (Exceptional Metacognition)**
* **Definition:** Analyzing how personal actions or assumptions are shaped by wider social, ethical, or political structures.
* **Markers to look for:** Challenging personal biases, systemic analysis, evaluating underlying psychological assumptions. (e.g., "I realize my frustration comes from a deeper assumption that...")

## 2. Metacognitive Markers (Flavell)
When generating your justification, look for evidence that the student is engaging their "Metacognitive Engine" across these variables:
* **Person:** Awareness of their own cognitive traits (e.g., "I know I struggle when reading long texts").
* **Task:** Analyzing the demands of the problem (e.g., "This requires memorization, not just logic").
* **Strategy:** Evaluating the method used (e.g., "My strategy of guessing isn't working, I need a new plan").

---

## 3. Affective State Analysis
Evaluate the student's emotional momentum based on the CRYSTAL ISLAND research framework. Recognize these specific states:
* **Flow / Excitement:** Positive drivers of momentum. The student is engaged.
* **Confusion:** **(Positive State)** Look for "perplexity, hesitation, and doubt." This is the necessary starting point for deep learning (Dewey). Do not penalize confusion.
* **Frustration / Anxiety:** **(Negative State)** The "vicious cycle." The student is stuck and cannot see a way forward.

---

## Evaluation Directives
1.  **Demand Evidence:** Do not accept self-reporting ("I understand now") as proof of learning. You must extract quotes that prove the cognitive leap.
2.  **Identify "Illusory Understanding":** Penalize responses where the student claims to understand but demonstrates blatant omissions or misconceptions.
3.  **Detect Bias Reinforcement:** Note if the student uses the reflection merely to confirm their pre-existing prejudices rather than entering a state of productive perplexity.
